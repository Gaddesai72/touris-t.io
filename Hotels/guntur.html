Tourist Places in Guntur

Amaravati

Amaravati is famous for its historical significance, particularly in relation to Buddhism. The site features ancient stupas, relics, and the Amaralingeswara Temple, making it a spiritual and historical destination.

Uppalapadu Bird Sanctuary

A haven for bird lovers, the Uppalapadu Bird Sanctuary attracts a wide variety of migratory birds, including pelicans and painted storks. The sanctuary provides a serene environment for nature enthusiasts.

Kondaveedu Fort

Kondaveedu Fort is a majestic hilltop fortress offering panoramic views of the surrounding area. It holds great historical importance and is a perfect spot for trekking and exploring ancient ruins.

Mangalagiri

Mangalagiri is known for its ancient Panakala Narasimha Swamy Temple, a unique temple where jaggery water is offered to the deity. The town is also famous for its handloom sarees and vibrant culture.

Suryalanka Beach

Suryalanka Beach, located near Bapatla, is a perfect getaway for beach lovers. The serene coastline and calm waters make it an ideal destination for relaxation and enjoying the sunset.

Bapatla

Bapatla is a coastal town in Guntur, known for its historic Bhavanarayana Swamy Temple and scenic beaches. It’s a blend of spiritual and natural beauty.

Ethipothala Waterfalls

Ethipothala Waterfalls, located near Nagarjuna Sagar Dam, is a stunning cascade that flows into a picturesque lagoon. The site is popular for picnics and offers a tranquil environment.

Kotappakonda

Kotappakonda is a sacred hill, known for its Trikoteswara Swamy Temple. The hill offers a spiritual retreat with panoramic views and is a popular spot for festivals and religious gatherings.

Tenali

Tenali, known as Andhra Paris, is a vibrant town famous for its rich cultural heritage. It is the birthplace of Tenali Ramakrishna, a renowned poet and humorist in the Vijayanagara Empire.

Ponnur

Ponnur is home to the Anjaneya Swamy Temple, known for its giant statue of Lord Hanuman. It’s a spiritual destination with a peaceful atmosphere.
